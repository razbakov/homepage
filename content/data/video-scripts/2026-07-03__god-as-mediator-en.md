# Daily — We Believe in God Because We Are Afraid to Understand Each Other

**Source:** https://razbakov.com/blog/2026-06-01-god-as-mediator-en

## Hook (0:00–0:20)

We split atoms. We built AI. And we still can't understand the person sitting across from us at dinner. Today's thought — what if we didn't turn to God because we fear death… but because we fear each other?

`[B-ROLL: two people sitting at a table in silence, coffee between them, neither making eye contact]`
`[TEXT ON SCREEN: "What if God is a middleman?"]`

## Point 1 — The mystery we avoid (0:20–1:10)

Here's what real understanding asks of you. Not knowledge — you can Google knowledge. Understanding asks you to let go of being right. To see through someone else's eyes. To accept that the person in front of you is as deep, as contradictory, as alive as you are.

And that terrifies us.

Because to understand another person means to let in their pain. Their mess. Their freedom. It's so much easier to turn them into a label. A category. An enemy. We say we love humanity — but loving a specific human, with all their flaws, right in front of you? That's the hard part.

So what do we do? We look up instead of across.

`[B-ROLL: crowd of strangers walking past each other on a busy street, faces blurred]`
`[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Easier to love humanity than to love a human."]`

## Point 2 — The middleman in the sky (1:10–2:10)

This is where it gets interesting. What if the idea of God — at least partly — works as a mediator between people? Instead of building bridges to each other, we build bridges to something above. God becomes the common language for people who've lost the ability to speak to one another directly.

Think about why religious arguments get so vicious. It's not really about theology. It's about whether human understanding is even possible. When people can't meet each other, they meet around symbols. When they can't share reality, they create a shared faith.

And I want to be clear — this doesn't mean God is a fiction. It means something subtler. Even if God exists, our image of God might be shaped by the human gap we're trying to cross. We pour into that image the hope for an understanding we can't find among ourselves.

`[B-ROLL: hands reaching toward stained glass light in a cathedral, then cut to two hands reaching toward each other]`
`[TEXT ON SCREEN: "We look up because we can't look across."]`

## Point 3 — The paradox (2:10–3:05)

Here's the twist that gets me. Almost every spiritual tradition says the same thing: the path to God runs through another person. Compassion. Forgiveness. Attention to someone else's life. That's the highest religious experience.

So if that's true — then our fear of understanding each other is also our fear of drawing close to God.

The hardest revelation isn't a voice from the sky. It's a stranger's gaze. Not a sacred text — an honest conversation. The question was never "does God exist." The question is: can we bear the encounter with each other?

And maybe — if we ever learn to truly understand one another — we'll either stop needing God as a middleman… or we'll find him exactly where we never thought to look. Right here. Between us.
