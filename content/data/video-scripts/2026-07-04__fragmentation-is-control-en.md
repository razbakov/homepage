**Source:** https://razbakov.com/blog/2026-05-31-fragmentation-is-control-en

## Hook (0:00–0:20)

Babel is not a punishment story. Read it again — the deity says, out loud, "nothing they propose will be impossible for them." Then he scrambles their language. That's not justice. That's a blueprint. Someone wrote down how control works three thousand years ago, and we're still running the same code.

## Point 1 — The Algorithm (0:20–1:10)

Here's the pattern. Take something that wants to be whole — a continent, a mind, a relationship — split it into pieces, and sell people the pieces back as identity. The British split Nigeria into "tribes" and governed by playing them against each other. Lord Lugard wrote a book about it. He wasn't hiding. American schooling split the mind into IQ and EQ, sorted children by which they scored higher, and called it meritocracy. The Prussian model it came from was designed — openly — to produce obedient workers. There's no smoking gun because the gun was the curriculum. David Graeber showed that precise, state-enforced debt requires constant violence. Police. Prisons. Armies. And the language of debt and the language of sin share a root. Redemption — that's a debt word. Bought back. Paid off. You can run this in every domain. Language, money, morality, knowledge, consciousness. Same move. Every time.

`[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Split what wants to be whole. Sell the pieces back as identity."]`

## Point 2 — No Conspirator Needed (1:10–2:10)

Now — this is where most people lose the plot. They notice the pattern and they draw a circle on the map and write THEY inside it. The Rothschilds. The Bilderbergs. The thirteen bloodlines. It's comforting to have a THEY. It puts a face on the machine. And it's wrong. Here's what took me years to see: the pattern doesn't need a conspirator. It's what any unsupervised system optimizes for. Foucault spent his life on this. Bourdieu spent his life on this. Gramsci spent his prison sentence on this. A teacher who's internalized "gifted kids" and "regular kids" doesn't need a memo from headquarters. A bank that's internalized "creditworthy" doesn't need a phone call from a cabal. The filters are inside the people running the institutions. Most of them are good people. The machine still runs. And that's worse than a conspiracy — because a conspiracy you could expose. You could leak documents, arrest someone. But there's no document. The document is the operating system, and the operating system is in everyone's head. Including yours. Including mine.

## Point 3 — Integration as Practice (2:10–3:05)

So what do you do? You integrate. Wherever you find a split that's been sold to you as nature — undo it personally. Then help one person near you undo it. Then build a small room where the unsplit version is the default. That's what every project I work on is. WeDance — salseros and kizombatas and swing kids in one room, no enforced borders. The market hates this. The market rewards the split because the split creates the addressable segment. The integrated thing has no segment. You build from zero. But here's what the integrated thing has that the fragmented thing never will: the people who find it stay. Because nobody who's tasted integration wants to go back to being addressed as a segment.
