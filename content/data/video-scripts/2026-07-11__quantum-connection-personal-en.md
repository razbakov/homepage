# Daily — I Have Connection to the Quantum World — But Not Influence (Yet)

**Source:** https://razbakov.com/blog/2026-05-08-quantum-connection-personal-en

## Hook (0:00–0:20)

Ideas show up in my head fully formed. Buildings, products, communities — they arrive like weather. I didn't ask for them. I don't control when they come. But here's what I've realized: receiving is not the same as broadcasting. And the gap between those two things? That's the entire game.

## Point 1 — The channel is open (0:20–1:10)

So let me explain what I mean by connection. I close my eyes and I see things. A website. A system of AI agents running a whole portfolio. A frame from a film I haven't made yet. These aren't fuzzy daydreams — they have weight. They feel less like imagination and more like reception. Like something that already exists somewhere is showing me a picture of itself.

For most of my life I thought everyone had this. They don't. Most people generate ideas the way you boil water — slowly, with effort. For me they just land. And what I can do is grab one and materialize it. Pour in time, money, attention, reputation. Vision plus resources equals something real. That part is almost mechanical.

But that's connection. That's not influence.

`[B-ROLL: hands sketching on paper, then cutting to a live website loading on screen]`
`[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Vision + resources = materialization"]`

## Point 2 — What influence actually is (1:10–2:10)

Influence — the kind I'm pointing at — is something fundamentally different. Influence is broadcasting into the channel. Not just receiving ideas, but planting them. Putting a vision into the world in a way that other people start receiving it independently. In their own time. In their own heads. Without you convincing them one by one.

Think about the founders and artists whose ideas seem to spread without paid distribution. When they speak, the thing they describe starts appearing in other people's minds. Not because of marketing. Because they're shaping the substrate everyone else is reading from.

I'm not there. I can capture. I can build. I can show what I'm building. But I can't plant an image in the collective layer and watch it bloom in twenty thousand people without lifting another finger. That resonance — that's the next mountain.

`[B-ROLL: ripple spreading across still water, then aerial shot of a crowd]`
`[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Influence = ideas that spread without you pushing"]`

## Point 3 — Staying honest at the foot of the mountain (2:10–3:05)

The temptation when you have any unusual capacity is to inflate it. Perform a higher altitude than you're actually at. I'm not interested in that. The metrics will tell on me anyway.

Right now, a handful of people read what I write. A small circle has started delegating decisions to me. That's real. But the ideas don't yet spread on their own. So I'm watching for the signals — strangers extending frameworks I publish. People quoting things back to me that I never told them directly. Volunteer momentum before I've recruited anyone. Those signals are starting. Slowly. The curve is moving.

And I think I know what raises it: keep capturing, keep shipping, keep being honest about where I am. The broadcast capacity comes from depth and consistency, not from declaring it.
