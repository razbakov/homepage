MysteryGames runs monthly social-deduction nights in Munich — Mafia reimagined as live theater. Every month, ten strangers gather in a secret room at a rotating venue, revealed 48 hours before, for a few rounds of bluffing, reading people, and figuring out who isn't who they say they are.

**Problem:** Meeting new people in a new city is hard, and most social events are either loud bars or awkward small talk. There's little that gives people a structured, playful reason to interact.

**Solution:** A recurring event brand where the game itself does the social work — no scripts, no rehearsals, just an evening of live deduction. The website carries the brand, lists the next night, and handles signups, turning a party game into a repeatable community event people come back to.
