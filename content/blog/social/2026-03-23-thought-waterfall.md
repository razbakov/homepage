## LinkedIn Post

I wake up at 3:45am with ideas crashing into each other faster than I can write them down.

I've experienced this for 10 years during free writing sessions. You dump thoughts on paper, read what you wrote, and it triggers new connections — each thought reinforcing the next like a waterfall.

But recently, working with AI sub-agents made it 10x more intense. I dispatch a prompt, and while waiting for the result, I start the next one. The visualization of how everything connects keeps accelerating. Ideas arrive faster than I can capture them.

It's not flow state. Flow is deep single-task focus. This is multi-thread convergence — many streams crashing together.

The risk: sleep disruption, no quality filter, fear of forgetting.
The opportunity: creative output that exceeds any structured brainstorming.
